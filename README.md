## About Me

I am a researcher with a Ph.D. in Computer Systems Architecture and more than 15 years of academic teaching and research experience. My work is focused on the intersection of classical data science, artificial intelligence, machine learning, signal processing, data security, and quantum computing.

I am particularly interested in developing methods for representing, processing, analyzing, and securing classical data within both classical and quantum computational models. My research activities include quantum machine learning, quantum signal processing, secure multimedia processing, audio steganography, watermarking, steganalysis, intelligent data-driven systems, and hybrid classical–quantum frameworks.

Through my research, I aim to build practical connections between classical AI and signal processing methods and the emerging capabilities of quantum computing, especially for applications that require accurate data analysis, security, scalability, robustness, and intelligent decision-making.
