## About Me
I am a Computer Engineering researcher with a strong academic and research background in computer systems, artificial intelligence, data science, signal processing, multimedia security, and quantum computing. I have more than 15 years of experience in academic teaching, scientific research, technical writing, and applied research projects.

My work focuses on the intersection of classical data science, artificial intelligence, machine learning, signal processing, data security, and quantum computing. I am particularly interested in developing intelligent, secure, and scalable computational methods for representing, processing, analyzing, and protecting classical data using both classical and quantum computational models.

My research activities include quantum machine learning, quantum signal processing, secure multimedia processing, audio steganography, watermarking, steganalysis, intelligent data-driven systems, optimization algorithms, and hybrid classical–quantum frameworks.

Through my research, I aim to build practical connections between classical artificial intelligence, signal processing, secure data analysis, and the emerging capabilities of quantum computing, especially for applications that require accuracy, scalability, robustness, security, privacy, and intelligent decision-making.
